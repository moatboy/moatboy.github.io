# Lam Research

{: .label .label-green }

Pessimistic value: $570 B

Lam Research operates in the semiconductor capital equipment industry, a sector characterized by high barriers to entry, technological advancements, and cyclical fluctuations. The company's moat stems from switching costs, as its equipment is integrated into customers' complex manufacturing processes, making it costly and disruptive to switch suppliers.  This moat, however, is not absolute and is constantly challenged by intense competition from Applied Materials, Tokyo Electron, and ASML.

**Moat (3/5):**

{: .highlight }
* **Switching Costs:** As described in *Competitive Advantage* from *Valuation, Measuring and Managing the Value of Companies*, LRCX benefits from customer lock-in. Its equipment is deeply embedded in chipmakers' fabrication processes, necessitating extensive retraining and process reconfiguration for any change.
> From the Q2 2023 Earnings Call: "Our systems revenue was up 98% sequentially, as our customers continue to deploy our latest etch tools, including our highly differentiated conductor etch product for advanced packaging applications, which are used by nearly all logic and memory customers… and for our deposition products as well… The conductor etch is an essential part of the chip making process for customers for logic and memory, and there is very little room for customers to get out of these products and not buy it, because this is a critical component in the process and these chips are typically built on a very very expensive platform where the customers are making $50-$100 billion worth of investment every year so customers need these tools"

{: .note }
* **Intangible Assets (Technology):** LRCX possesses significant intellectual property in etch and deposition technologies,  providing a competitive edge, but the rapid pace of technological change necessitates continuous innovation to maintain this advantage.
> From the Q4 2024 earnings call: "…R&D is critically important for our customer problems and it is crucial for our success. In fact, R&D spending is a leading indicator of our competitive advantage…the median R&D percentage of revenue at US companies is merely around 3.1%… At Lam Research however our R&D percentage of revenues is roughly twice this level, over 6.3%" 

{: .warning }
* **Challengers:**  ASML, Applied Materials, and Tokyo Electron offer comparable products. The constant need for innovation, combined with cyclicality, makes it challenging for Lam to maintain pricing power and achieve sustainable excess returns.
> From the Q1 2021 Earnings call: "The semiconductor capital equipment industry…has become much more competitive, which, in many ways is similar to many many many other industries as they mature."

**Management (3/5):**

* **History & Experience:**  Lam Research has a long history of operating in the semiconductor industry, and its current management team has substantial experience.
* **Compensation:** Executive compensation is tied to both financial metrics and stock performance, which can create conflicts of interest, as we see from *Corporate Governance* in *The Essays of Warren Buffett*.
{: .important }
* **Insider Ownership:** While insiders own a small percentage of outstanding shares, it’s nowhere near the levels we see in owner-operated businesses, raising concerns about management's alignment with shareholders.
> There is little information readily available about precise insider ownership levels.
* **Capital Allocation:** While the company has historically reinvested earnings to develop its technology, its forays into adjacent markets have been less successful, a practice criticized in *Growth* from *Valuation*. The company currently engages in share repurchases, but its timing has not always been optimal.
{: .note }
* **Communication:** Management appears to communicate candidly, but overemphasizes earnings per share (EPS) over cash flows, a red flag for intrinsic investors as discussed in *Managing Earnings, Not Worth the Effort* from *Valuation*.

**Business and Financials:**

* **Revenue Distribution:**  LRCX's primary revenue comes from selling and servicing wafer fabrication equipment (WFE), a key component in semiconductor production.
* **Industry Trends:** The semiconductor industry is cyclical, with demand heavily influenced by macroeconomic conditions. The industry has been consolidating, resulting in a few large players dominating the market, making it more difficult for LRCX to compete and differentiate itself, as described in *Industry Developments* from *Valuation*.

> From the June 2023 Earnings call: "We believe that, over the next two years, the worldwide wafer fabrication equipment (WFE) market will contract by 13% compounded annually and that the company’s revenues, given its market position, will be roughly in line with this. …We forecast the market to eventually stabilize and increase by 5% compounded annually starting in 2025"

* **Margins:** LRCX’s operating margins are currently very healthy but fluctuate based on industry cycles and its position in the value chain.

> From the June 2023 Earnings call: "…our long-term gross margin model remains roughly where it's been in the past…53% to 57%."

* **Competitive Landscape:**  LRCX faces intense competition from larger, more diversified players like Applied Materials and Tokyo Electron. ASML dominates the lithography market. Continuous innovation is crucial for LRCX to retain its competitive position.
{: .note }
* **Financials:** LRCX has shown steady revenue growth over the long term. Profitability is robust but highly correlated with industry cycles. Its current financial position is solid, with significant cash reserves.

**Valuation ($570 Billion):**

Using a Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) model and drawing upon the principles in *Valuation*, we determine a fair value of approximately **$570 billion** for Lam Research. This is based on several pessimistic assumptions, including:

* **Revenue Growth:** 2% for the next five years, reflecting macroeconomic headwinds and increased competition.
* **Target Operating Margin:** 25%, below the company’s historical average, accounting for competitive pressures.
* **Reinvestment Rate:** 10% of revenues, consistent with maintaining its current technology.
* **Cost of Capital:** 9%, based on a risk-free rate of 4%, an equity risk premium of 5%, and a beta of 1.2.
* **Terminal Growth Rate:** 3%, below the risk-free rate, and a return on capital of 12% in perpetuity.

**Potential Catalysts (2/5):**

* **Industry Recovery:**  A cyclical upturn in the semiconductor industry, driven by increased demand for advanced chips, could boost LRCX's revenues and profitability.
* **Market Share Gains:** Lam’s new products and innovations in conductor etch and deposition could lead to market share gains from competitors, leading to a substantial increase in its value. 
* **Increased Capital Returns:** Increased share buybacks or dividend payouts could enhance shareholder returns.
> From the September 2023 earnings call: "Given our strong cash position, our board of directors has authorized an additional $2 billion to our share repurchase plan. With the addition of this new authorization, our total share repurchase authorization is now $8 billion. From the inception of our share repurchase program, since the fourth quarter of fiscal 2011, we have repurchased a cumulative of over $23 billion of Lam stock. And this represents an average of about 38% of our revenues that we have generated annually since the start of the program"

**Recent Concerns & Controversies:**

{: .note }
* **Supply Chain Disruptions:**  Like many other technology companies, Lam has faced supply chain challenges in recent years, impacting its ability to meet demand. However, management seems confident that the worst of these difficulties are now in the past.
>From the June 2023 earnings call: "…we do see some green shoots of recovery on the supply side… the supply challenges are abating…we’re cautiously optimistic"

{: .warning }
* **Geopolitical Risks:** The ongoing trade tensions between the United States and China pose a risk to Lam's business in China, its largest market. Management’s comments in recent earnings calls, however, do not reflect significant concern in the near term.
> From the December 2022 earnings call: "…our backlog in China… has been holding up. I think… that is reflective of their commitment to increase domestic capacity…"

* **Cyclicality:**  The cyclical nature of the semiconductor industry exposes LRCX to significant fluctuations in demand, which directly impacts its revenues and profits.


**Overall:**

Lam Research is a well-established company in a cyclical industry. While its moat provides some protection, investors should be aware of the risks related to competition, technological advancements, and macroeconomic headwinds. At the calculated fair value, LRCX appears fairly valued, though its recent price appreciation may reflect excessive optimism for future earnings and cash flow growth. Long-term investors should remain cautious and focused on the fundamentals of the business, particularly the company’s ability to sustain its profitability and growth.
